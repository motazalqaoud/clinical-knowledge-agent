## First-line pharmacologic therapy

For most adults with newly diagnosed hypertension, an ACE inhibitor or
a calcium channel blocker is a reasonable first-line choice, absent
contraindications. A typical starting regimen is Lisinopril 10mg PO QD,
which may be titrated to a maximum of Lisinopril 40mg PO QD as
tolerated. An alternative first-line option is Amlodipine 5mg PO QD,
titrated up to 10mg PO QD.

## Blood pressure targets

For most adults, a target blood pressure < 130/80 mmHg is recommended.
A less stringent target of < 140/90 mmHg may be appropriate for
patients over 65 with significant frailty or limited life expectancy.

## Combination therapy

If blood pressure remains above goal after four weeks of monotherapy,
Hydrochlorothiazide 25mg PO QD may be added. Triple therapy combining
an ACE inhibitor, a calcium channel blocker, and a thiazide diuretic is
reserved for resistant hypertension not controlled on two agents.

## Monitoring frequency

Home blood pressure monitoring BID (once in the morning, once in the
evening) is recommended during the first few weeks of therapy or after
any dose adjustment. Follow-up visits are typically scheduled every 2-4
weeks until blood pressure is at goal, then every 3-6 months thereafter.
A basic metabolic panel should be checked within 1-2 weeks of starting
or adjusting an ACE inhibitor, ARB, or diuretic, to monitor potassium
and creatinine.

## Hypertensive urgency

A blood pressure reading > 180/120 mmHg without symptoms of acute organ
damage should be rechecked after 15-30 minutes of rest. If still
elevated, oral antihypertensive therapy should be initiated or adjusted,
with follow-up PRN within 24-72 hours. Same-day emergency evaluation is
warranted if symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, or
neurologic changes are present.

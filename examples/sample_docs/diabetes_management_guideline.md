## First-line pharmacologic therapy

Metformin remains the preferred initial pharmacologic agent for most
adults with newly diagnosed type 2 diabetes, absent contraindications
such as significant renal impairment. A typical starting regimen is
Metformin 500mg PO BID, taken with meals to reduce gastrointestinal
side effects. The dose may be titrated upward over several weeks to a
maximum of Metformin 1000mg PO BID as tolerated.

## Glycemic targets

For most non-pregnant adults, target HbA1c < 7.0% is recommended.
A less stringent goal of HbA1c < 8.0% may be appropriate for patients
with a history of severe hypoglycemia, limited life expectancy, or
extensive comorbid conditions. Fasting plasma glucose targets are
generally 80-130 mg/dL.

## Insulin initiation

When oral therapy alone is insufficient to reach glycemic targets,
basal insulin may be added. A common starting dose is insulin glargine
10 units QHS (at bedtime), titrated based on fasting glucose readings.
Rapid-acting insulin, when needed at mealtimes, is often dosed as
insulin aspart 4 units TID before meals, adjusted per carbohydrate
intake and correction scale.

## Monitoring frequency

Self-monitoring of blood glucose is typically recommended QID (four
times daily: before each meal and at bedtime) during initial insulin
titration, decreasing to BID or QD once the regimen is stable.
Laboratory HbA1c should be rechecked every three months until at goal,
then every six months thereafter.

## Hypoglycemia management

Patients experiencing symptomatic hypoglycemia should be instructed to
treat PRN with 15-20 grams of fast-acting carbohydrate, rechecking
blood glucose after 15 minutes and repeating as needed.
